**Phishing**:

phishing is a type of cyberattack where attackers impersonate trusted entities---such as banks, colleagues, or popular websites---via email, text, or phone to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data. These fraudulent messages often create a false sense of urgency or fear to compel victims to click malicious links or download infected attachments.

**Key Aspects of Phishing Attacks:**

• Method: Primarily distributed through email, but also includes SMS (smishing), voice calls (vishing), and social media messages.

• Goal: To steal login credentials, financial information, or deploy malware (such as ransomware) to gain unauthorized access to systems or commit identity theft.

• Deception Tactics: Attackers use spoofed, nearly identical web pages and email addresses that mimic legitimate brands to lower the victim\'s guard.

• Targeting: While many attacks are widespread, \"spear phishing\" targets specific individuals with customized, more convincing messages.

**Case Study: Online Banking Phishing Scam**

In 2023, a mid-level professional received what appeared to be an official email from his bank, warning him of "suspicious activity" on his account. The crime in this case is phishing---a type of cyber fraud where attackers impersonate legitimate institutions to steal sensitive information such as login credentials or financial data.

**Targets**:

targets are often individuals with active online banking accounts, especially those who may not be highly tech-savvy or who respond quickly to urgent messages. Elderly individuals and busy professionals are common victims.

**Consequences**:

The consequences can be severe. Victims may suffer financial loss, identity theft, and emotional distress. Additionally, resolving such cases often requires lengthy interactions with banks and authorities, and full recovery of lost funds is not always guaranteed. This case highlights the importance of digital awareness and verifying communications before taking action.
